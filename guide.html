🏓 Rumble Pickleball

A free web app that runs your whole pickleball tournament — Round Robin, Stack, King of the Court, Gauntlet & Crown. Use 4–24 players on 1–6 courts, no app store, no account, works offline. Here's how to get it on any device and run your first event.

Link zigrivers.github.io/rumble-pickleball
01

Get it on your device

Pick your device. It saves to your home screen / Dock and opens like a normal app — no download.

📱

iPhone & iPad

Use Safari — not Chrome
  1. Open the link in Safari.
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with an ↑ arrow, bottom center).
  3. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. Tap Add (top-right corner).
  5. Open the Rumble icon on your home screen.
🤖

Android

Use Chrome
  1. Open the link in Chrome.
  2. Tap the ⋮ menu (three dots, top-right).
  3. Tap Add to Home screen (or Install app).
  4. Tap Add / Install to confirm.
  5. Open the Rumble icon from your apps.
💻

Mac

Safari 17+ · or Chrome/Edge
  1. Open the link in Safari.
  2. In the menu bar: File → Add to Dock… (or Share → Add to Dock).
  3. Keep the name Rumble, click Add.
  4. Launch it from your Dock — it opens in its own window.
  5. Chrome/Edge: click the install icon (a screen with ↓) in the address bar → Install.
🪟

Windows PC

Use Chrome or Edge
  1. Open the link in Chrome or Edge.
  2. Chrome: click the install icon (a monitor with ↓) on the right side of the address bar.
  3. Edge: click ··· → Apps → Install this site as an app.
  4. Click Install.
  5. Launch Rumble from your Start menu / desktop.
💡 No install needed. You can also just bookmark the link and use it in any browser tab — installing only adds the standalone window + icon. Either way it works offline after the first visit and auto-saves your tournament.
02

Run your first tournament

Three plays: set up, score each round, crown your champions.

Play 1

Set up

  1. Pick a Format card — Round Robin, Stack, King of the Court, Gauntlet, or Crown Court (4 players).
  2. Choose your court count, add or remove roster rows, then type a name in each player box. Tip: tap Paste names to drop in a whole list at once.
  3. Optional: flip on Mixed mode to pair 1 man + 1 woman per team every round. Each player gets an M / W toggle; labels are editable if your groups aren't men/women.
  4. Optional: add a phone number to text someone their results later.
  5. Optional: flip on Set a time budget — it tunes the scoring to fit your clock.
  6. Tap Start Tournament, review the summary, then start and watch the courts get drawn.
Play 2

Score each round

  1. Each court shows the two teams and a score box.
  2. Type each team's final score — or tap the × 11 pill to fill the winner instantly, then type the loser's.
  3. Live Standings update as you go; a timer tracks each court.
  4. When all active courts are done, tap Round 2 →.
  5. Need a fix? Use the Undo chip or Event Log, or tap ← Round N to edit any earlier score.
Play 3

Crown the champions

  1. After the last round, tap Build Finals →.
  2. Enter the finals tier scores — Championship first, then the lower tiers.
  3. Tap 👑 Crown Champions for the podium, awards & final standings.
  4. Added phones? Use 📱 Text results to share each recap.
  5. Use Share Cards or QR Snapshot for a polished podium card or static standings link.
  6. Tap Start New Tournament for round two of the day.
03

More players & courts

Rumble now scales most formats beyond the classic 8-player, 2-court setup.

👥
Use 4–24 players. Round Robin, Stack, King, and Gauntlet all support flexible roster sizes. Crown Court stays exactly 4 players on one court.
🏟
Use 1–6 courts. Rumble fills as many four-player courts as the roster can support; extra configured courts wait until enough players are active.
☕
Byes are automatic. If the roster does not divide cleanly into courts of four, the app lists who is sitting each round and rotates byes fairly.
🔁
Adjust during play. In Settings, Manage Players can add late arrivals, mark someone left, return them later, or change court count for the next round.
🤝
Mixed mode. Turn it on in setup to always pair 1 man + 1 woman per team. Works across Round Robin, Stack, King, and Gauntlet. On a lopsided roster it maximizes mixed teams and shows a Why? on any same-group fallback.
04

Good to know

📺
Use TV / Projector Mode. Open ⚙️ Settings → TV / Projector Mode for a read-only big-screen view, and keep Keep display awake on when casting.
💾
It auto-saves. Close the window and come back later — your tournament is right where you left it.
📶
Works offline. Once you've opened it once, no signal needed at the courts.
↶
Recover from mistakes. Recent score, round, finals, roster, and court changes appear in the Event Log and can be undone when reversible.
?
Tap Why? Bye banners, ladder movement, and finals seeds explain the rule Rumble used for that decision.
🤝
Share it. Send the app link, export result cards, or show a QR Snapshot so players can open a static standings view.
🔎
Make it courtside-readable. In ⚙️ Settings → Readability, turn on Large score entry for bigger score buttons, High-contrast court labels so courts read by text not just color, and Compact mobile view to focus the current round in Score / Standings / More tabs on a phone.
🧹
Quiet the standings. Reduce visual noise hides partner chips, badges, trajectory arrows, and advanced columns — the ranking order never changes. Fine-grained toggles let you bring any one back.
⚡
Set up fast. On the setup screen, tap a Quick template (Lunch Break 45, Club Night 90, Competitive Ladder, Social Mixer) or open the Setup Assistant for recommendations by player count, courts, time, and goal. Both just fill in the normal controls — edit anything afterward.
📱
One device runs the show. Multi-device score entry (each court on its own phone) isn't available yet — score from the organizer's device for now.
